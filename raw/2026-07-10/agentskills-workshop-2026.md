# Agent Skills '26 — First CAIS Workshop on Agent Skills

**Source:** https://www.agentskills-workshop.org/  
**Type:** Academic workshop (ACM CAIS 2026)  
**Date observed:** 2026-07-10  
**Signal rating:** 9/10 (first dedicated academic venue for Agent Skills)

## Key facts

- Full-day workshop: May 26, 2026 · San Jose, CA (ACM CAIS)
- Theme: Design, Evaluation, and Optimization of Procedural Knowledge for LLM Agents
- **103 submissions**, **45 accepted posters**, **6 oral presentations** (non-archival)
- Skills defined as structured packages of instructions, scripts, and references (`SKILL.md`) adopted across Claude Code, Gemini CLI, OpenClaw, Codex, and 30+ platforms
- Field stats cited by organizers:
  - 8 focused Skills papers in Feb–Mar 2026
  - Audit of 3,984 Skills: **37% contained security flaws**
  - SkillsBench: curated Skills improve pass rates by **+16.2 points** average; self-generated Skills can *hurt* performance in nearly 1/3 of tasks
- After party: SkillsBench 1.0 Launch Party (Google DeepMind, BenchFlow, Kaggle, Kernel Labs) — May 27, San Francisco

## Topics of interest (lifecycle framing)

1. Applications — harnesses, design principles, composition & scaling
2. Evaluation & Benchmarking — Skills as first-class artifacts
3. Safety & Supply Chain — malicious detection, formal verification, prompt injection via Skills
4. Improvement & Learning — LLM generation, RL, evolutionary approaches, trajectory mining
