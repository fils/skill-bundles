# Inside the Skill Market: SE Activities as Reusable Agent Skills

**Source:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09065 | https://arxiv.org/html/2607.09065v1
**Date:** Jul 10 2026 | Cao, Yan, Chen, Lu, Liu, Cheung (HKUST et al.)
**Signal rating:** 8/10

## Key ideas
- First large-scale activity-centric study of **SE skills** in marketplaces: 11,497 SE-related skills.
- Question: which SE lifecycle activities are encapsulated as reusable skills?
- Findings: SE activities themselves becoming reusable artifacts; uneven lifecycle coverage (construction/coding heavy; requirements/design underrepresented).
- Skill versioning analysis (ClawHub): most skills single-version (969); max 72 versions; version count ≠ maturity indicator.
- Five-component skill model: Metadata, Instructions, Resources, optional executables, supplementary docs.
- Calls for skill recommendation, engineering-oriented structuring, high-context activity encapsulation, better versioning mechanisms.

## Why skill-bundles
SE-domain counterpart to registry→repository study; concrete evidence for versioning/compatibility gap (SWE-Skills-Bench tomorrow-list item).
