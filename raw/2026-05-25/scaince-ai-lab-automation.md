## Abstract Summary
A review merging the 2024 World Economic Forum Top-10-Technologies Report with latest advances through mid-2025, charting the course from automated literature synthesis and hypothesis generation to self-driving laboratories, organoid intelligence, and climate-scale forecasting. Grounded in the EU AI Act and ISO 42001 governance frameworks.

## Key Thesis
Research is entering a "co-pilot to lab-pilot" transition where AI no longer merely interprets knowledge but increasingly **acts upon it** — orchestrating cloud software and physical laboratory hardware with multimodal agentic systems.

## Core Sections & Findings

### 2. AI and Large Language Models for Scientific Discovery
#### 2.1 Automated Literature Review and Meta-Analysis
- AI-driven systems process and analyze vast published research at unprecedented speeds
- Platforms like Insilica's SysRev (16,000+ systematic review projects in 5 years), Iris.ai, Elicit.ai, Semantic Scholar
- PubMed uses AI for Best Match, query manipulation, author name disambiguation, automatic indexing
- LLM-centric pipeline (Doneva et al., 2024): screened 500+ pharmacogenomics papers, 10-point F1 improvement over rule-based, completed in under a week

#### 2.2 Hypothesis Generation
- LLMs integrate diverse data types, enabling interdisciplinary research
- Examples: ALS gene identification (Placek et al., 2021), cancer treatment planning (O'Connor & McVeigh, 2025)
- AlphaFold-2 (Jumper et al., 2021) predicted protein folds across whole proteomes with near-experimental accuracy

### 3. Generative AI in Experimental Design
- AI systems propose experiments, choose parameters, and optimize protocols
- Self-driving laboratories can now run 24/7 with minimal human intervention

### 4. Autonomous Agents & Self-Driving Laboratories
- Multimodal agentic systems that "listen, see, speak and act"
- Orchestrating cloud software and physical lab hardware
- Examples include autonomous chemistry labs, materials discovery platforms

### 5. Organoid Intelligence & Specialized Domains
- Brain organoids as potential biological computing substrates
- Predictive toxicology using microphysiological systems

### 6. Climate-Scale Forecasting
- AI models for weather and climate prediction at unprecedented resolution

### 7. AI Governance Frameworks
- EU AI Act and ISO 42001 as emerging regulatory infrastructure
- Concerns about reproducibility, auditability, safety, and equitable access
