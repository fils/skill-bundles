## Abstract — Summary
Until recently, the conversation about generative AI in science revolved around the textual prowess of LLMs (GPT-3.5, etc.). Now we are in the era of **multimodal agentic systems** that listen, see, speak, and act — orchestrating cloud software and physical laboratory hardware. This review merges the 2024 World Economic Forum Top-10-Technologies Report white paper with advances through mid-2025, covering: automated literature synthesis, hypothesis generation, self-driving laboratories, organoid intelligence, and climate-scale forecasting. Grounded in EU AI Act and ISO 42001 governance. The shift is "co-pilot to lab-pilot" — AI no longer merely interprets knowledge but increasingly **acts upon it**. Dramatic efficiency gains come with amplified concerns about reproducibility, auditability, safety, and equitable access.

**Keywords:** generative artificial intelligence, scientific discovery, self-driving laboratories, scAInce paradigm, predictive toxicology, microphysiological systems, AI governance

### 1. Introduction — WEF Top 10 Emerging Technologies (2024)

### 2. AI & LLMs for Scientific Discovery
Main domains:
- **Automated literature synthesis** — AI-driven literature review, pattern/trend/anomaly identification in vast datasets. SysRev enabled 16,000+ systematic review projects in 5 years. Iris.ai, Elicit.ai, Semantic Scholar, ORKG ASK platform, PubMed AI.
- **Meta-analysis** — LLM-centric pipeline screened & extracted 500+ pharmacogenomics papers, F1 scores 10 points above rule-based, completed in under a week (Doneva et al., 2024).
- **Hypothesis generation** — LLM integration of diverse data types from varied sources; interdisciplinary growth; subtle correlations/pattern identification; novel hypotheses. Examples: ALS gene discovery (Placek et al., 2021); AlphaFold-2 (Jumper et al., 2021).

### 3. Agents & Tool Use
Multimodal agents that can plan, execute, and validate across multiple modalities. This maps directly to **agent skills** — capabilities that agents compose to perform scientific workflows.

### 4. Lab Automation & Self-Driving Laboratories
Physical lab automation: robotic systems executing experiments without human intervention. The "scAInce" paradigm (science + AI).

### 5. Organoid Intelligence
Biological computing using engineered human brain cells.

### 6. Climate-Scale Forecasting
AI models for weather and climate prediction.

### 7. Challenges & Risks
- Reproducibility & auditability concerns
- Equitable access issues
- Governance frameworks (EU AI Act, ISO 42001)
