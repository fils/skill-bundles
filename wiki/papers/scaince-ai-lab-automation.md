## Summary

A comprehensive review charting the evolution of generative AI in science — from LLM-powered literature review to multimodal **agentic systems** that orchestrate cloud software and physical lab hardware. The author co-drafted the WEF 2024 "Top 10 Emerging Technologies" chapter on "AI for Scientific Discovery" and here merges that white paper with advances through mid-2025. Core thesis: research is in a **"co-pilot to lab-pilot" transition** where AI no longer merely interprets knowledge but increasingly **acts upon it**.

### Key Domains Covered:

1. **Automated Literature Synthesis** — AI-driven systems processing vast published research at speeds unachievable by humans. Platforms: SysRev (16,000+ systematic review projects), Iris.ai, Elicit.ai, Semantic Scholar, ORKG ASK, PubMed AI tools.

2. **Hypothesis Generation** — LLMs integrating diverse data types, identifying subtle correlations, proposing novel hypotheses. Examples: ALS gene identification (sCCA/wPRS), cancer treatment planning via AI, AlphaFold-2 protein structure prediction.

3. **Self-Driving Laboratories** — Autonomous systems executing experiments end-to-end. The "scAInce" paradigm (science + AI) encompasses AI hypothesis generation → experimental design → execution → analysis.

4. **Organoid Intelligence** — Brain organoids as biological computing substrates.

5. **Climate-Scale Forecasting** — AI models for weather/climate at unprecedented resolution.
